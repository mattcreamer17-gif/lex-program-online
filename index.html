The hypothesis

The same context layer is likely being rebuilt three times.

Every new regulation pulls an update through all four products. Every new AI feature risks becoming a separate integration rather than a shared capability. LEX Blackrose in particular asks the company to run two sales motions at once, one to web and marketing ops on LEX 4Web, one to CISOs and SOC leads on Blackrose.

A working view of where the work actually sits, so the language model earns its keep only where it belongs:

60%
Traditional code and database. Consent records, whistleblower intake, audit trails, multilingual content pipelines across LEX 4Web, LEX Canal, and Canal Antibullying.
30%
Rule-based logic. Directive mappings, ISO clauses, jurisdictional carve-outs. The part a lawyer-built product already does well, codified.
10%
Where a language model earns its keep. Triage of whistleblower reports in LEX Canal, first-draft DPIAs inside LEX 4Audit, natural-language querying of LEX Blackrose findings.

What the shared layer saves.

One orchestration layer, versioned, interpretable, sits above the four products. New regulation lands, the rules layer updates once. New AI feature ships, it lands in one place and the four products inherit. Headcount that is not there does not have to be hired.

The paper behind this is Interpretable Context Methodology (ICM, submitted to ACM TiiS). The repository is public: github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-. MIT licensed. Worth reading on a Donostia train ride.
